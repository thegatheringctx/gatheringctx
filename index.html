What drives us

Four convictions.
Not programs.

This is not just another gathering.

We do not gather hoping to win someday. We gather standing in what Christ has already finished. Past tense. Already done. Already won.

01
First Conviction
We will proclaim Christ. Fearlessly.

Not a watered-down Jesus designed to make everyone comfortable. The full Christ, crucified for sin, buried in a tomb, risen on the third day, seated at the right hand of the Father, and coming back in glory. In a world that wants to reduce Jesus to a life coach, we will declare Him as Lord.

02
Second Conviction
We will make disciples. Not just converts.

Jesus never said go make church members. He said go make disciples. Men and women who learn His ways, carry His authority, and reflect His character in every room they walk into. Formation over information. Transformation over attendance.

03
Third Conviction
We will build real community.

Not a crowd that sits in the same room once a week. A family. People who know each other's names, carry each other's burdens, celebrate each other's breakthroughs, and refuse to let each other drift. Real community is a supernatural witness to a lonely, fragmented world.

04
Fourth Conviction
We will send equipped witnesses into every sphere.

Teachers into classrooms. Doctors into hospitals. Parents into homes. Business owners into boardrooms. Artists into culture. Every believer, in every space they occupy, carrying the presence and the proclamation of Jesus Christ. Where you are, there the Mission Field is.

"He disarmed the rulers and authorities and put them to open shame, triumphing over them in the cross."

Colossians 2:15

We are the Church.
Christ is our foundation. His Word is our authority.
His Spirit is our power. And we walk with victory, not to it.

This is a launching pad for victory.

What we believe

Statement
of Faith.

Adopted by the Board of Directors of Gathering in Victory Inc. March 9, 2026

I · The Holy Scriptures
The Holy Scriptures

The Holy Bible, sixty-six books of the Old and New Testaments, is the inspired, inerrant, and infallible Word of God. Scripture alone is the supreme and final authority in all matters of faith and practice.

"All Scripture is breathed out by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness."2 Timothy 3:16–17

II · The Nature of God
The Nature of God

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three Persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, co-equal, co-eternal, co-substantial. Creator and Sustainer of all things, worthy of all worship.

"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit."Matthew 28:19

III · Jesus Christ
Jesus Christ

The eternal Son of God, born of a virgin, lived sinlessly, died as substitutionary atonement, raised bodily on the third day, ascended to the Father, returning in power and glory.

"Christ died for our sins in accordance with the Scriptures, that he was buried, that he was raised on the third day in accordance with the Scriptures."1 Corinthians 15:3–4

IV · The Holy Spirit
The Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit regenerates, seals, indwells, sanctifies, and empowers every believer. All gifts of the Holy Spirit described in the New Testament are active and available to the Church today.

"The Helper, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in my name, he will teach you all things and bring to your remembrance all that I have said to you."John 14:26

V · Humanity & Sin
Humanity & Sin

Humanity was created in the image of God. Through Adam's disobedience, all people are born with a sinful nature, in need of redemption only possible through faith in Jesus Christ.

"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, and are justified by his grace as a gift, through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus."Romans 3:23–24

VI · Salvation
Salvation

Salvation is by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone. The gift of God, received through genuine repentance. All who believe are justified, born again, and granted eternal life.

"For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast."Ephesians 2:8–9

VII · Baptism
Baptism

Baptism by immersion as a public declaration of faith and identification with Christ's death, burial, and resurrection. For believers only, an act of obedience and testimony of transformation.

"We were buried therefore with him by baptism into death, in order that, just as Christ was raised from the dead by the glory of the Father, we too might walk in newness of life."Romans 6:3–4

VIII · The Church
The Church

A covenant community of baptized believers gathering for worship, discipleship, fellowship, prayer, and Gospel proclamation. Governed by elders, served by deacons.

"They devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching and the fellowship, to the breaking of bread and the prayers."Acts 2:42

IX · The Lord's Supper
The Lord's Supper

A memorial ordinance instituted by Jesus Christ, proclaiming His death until He comes. Observed by baptized believers as remembrance of Christ's sacrifice.

"For as often as you eat this bread and drink the cup, you proclaim the Lord's death until he comes."1 Corinthians 11:26

X · Eschatology
Eschatology

We believe in the personal, visible, bodily return of Jesus Christ, the resurrection of the dead, final judgment, and eternal state, held with humility and conviction.

"The Lord himself will descend from heaven with a cry of command, with the voice of an archangel, and with the sound of the trumpet of God."1 Thessalonians 4:16

XI · Marriage & Family
Marriage & Family

Marriage is a covenant ordained by God, exclusively between one man and one woman. Human life is sacred from conception to natural death. Every person bears the image of God.

"Therefore a man shall leave his father and his mother and hold fast to his wife, and they shall become one flesh."Genesis 2:24

Generosity

Give with
purpose.

Everything we do is made possible by generosity. Your gift advances the Kingdom in Cleburne and beyond.

"Each of you should give what you have decided in your heart to give, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver." 2 Corinthians 9:7
